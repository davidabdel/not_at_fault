## 01 Brand Foundation

Not at Fault Claims is Australia's specialist in handling accident repairs for drivers who were not responsible for their collision. We exist to remove the stress, confusion, and unfair cost burden from innocent drivers — and to make the entire repair and claims process simple, transparent, and free for them.

### Our Mission

To make the not-at-fault claims process effortless for every Australian driver — handling everything from repair to insurance, so our customers never pay a cent they shouldn't.

### Our Vision

To be the most trusted name in not-at-fault smash repair across Australia, known for treating every customer with honesty, care, and speed.

### Our Promise

No excess. No hassle. No out-of-pocket cost. Just expert repairs, a free loan car, and a team that handles everything on your behalf.

### Core Brand Values

| Value | What It Means |
|-------|--------------|
| **Trust** | We are honest, transparent, and on our customer's side — always. |
| **Simplicity** | We remove complexity. Our process should feel effortless. |
| **Advocacy** | We fight for innocent drivers. We are their champion. |
| **Expertise** | We know this process inside-out, so our customers don't have to. |
| **Care** | Every customer has just had a stressful experience. We treat them with empathy. |

## 02 Brand Personality

Our brand personality is built around being the calm, confident expert in the room — the person who has seen this a hundred times and knows exactly what to do. We are warm without being casual, authoritative without being intimidating.

### We Are...

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Trustworthy** | We say what we mean and do what we say. |
| **Calm** | Accidents are stressful. We are the reassuring voice that makes it manageable. |
| **Clear** | We communicate in plain English. No jargon, no fine print surprises. |
| **Empathetic** | We acknowledge the customer's situation before jumping into process. |

### Brand Tagline

> *"Not your fault. Not your problem."*

This tagline captures our core proposition in one line — we take the burden completely off the innocent driver. Use it consistently across all touchpoints.

## 03 Logo Usage

The Not at Fault Claims logo is our most important brand asset. It must always appear clearly, with proper spacing, and in approved colour combinations only.

### Logo Versions

| Version | Background | Use Case |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Primary** | White / Light backgrounds | Default usage — website, print, documents |
| **Reverse** | Charcoal / Dark backgrounds | Dark mode, dark overlays, merchandise |

### Wordmark Structure

```
✓  NOT AT FAULT
   CLAIMS
```

- `NOT AT FAULT` — Bebas Neue Bold, Charcoal `#1C1C1C`
- `CLAIMS` — Bebas Neue Bold, Signal Orange `#FF5C00`
- Tick/shield icon — Signal Orange `#FF5C00`

### Clear Space Rule

Always maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "C" in CLAIMS. Nothing should encroach on this space — no text, images, or other brand marks.

### Minimum Size

| Medium | Minimum Size |
|--------|-------------|
| Print | 30mm wide |
| Digital / Screen | 120px wide |

Below these sizes, legibility is compromised and the logo must not be used.

### Logo Do's & Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| Use approved logo files only | Stretch, distort, or rotate the logo |
| Maintain clear space around the logo | Change any colours in the logo |
| Use on white, light grey, or charcoal backgrounds | Add effects: drop shadows, outlines, glows |
| Scale proportionally — always lock aspect ratio | Place on busy photographic backgrounds |
| Use the correct version for light vs dark backgrounds | Recreate or retype the logo in any font |

## 04 Colour Palette

Our colour palette is clean and purposeful. Orange signals action and confidence. Charcoal provides authority and trust. White space creates clarity and calm.

### Primary Palette

| Swatch | Name | Hex | Usage |
|--------|------|-----|-------|
| 🟧 | **Signal Orange** | `#FF5C00` | Primary brand colour — CTAs, accents, logo |
| ⬛ | **Charcoal** | `#1C1C1C` | Headlines, body text, dark backgrounds |
| ⬜ | **White** | `#FFFFFF` | Primary background — breathing room |

### Secondary Palette

| Swatch | Name | Hex | Usage |
|--------|------|-----|-------|
| 🔲 | **Light Grey** | `#F4F4F4` | Section backgrounds, card fills |
| 🔳 | **Mid Grey** | `#6B6B6B` | Captions, metadata, secondary text |
| 🟥 | **Pale Orange** | `#FFF0E8` | Info box backgrounds, subtle highlights |
| 🟩 | **Confirmation Green** | `#00A86B` | Success states, tick icons |

### Colour Rules

- **Signal Orange** is used sparingly — CTAs, the logo word "CLAIMS", key callouts. Never use as a body text background.
- **Charcoal** is the default for all headlines and body text.
- **White** dominates all layouts. Generous white space is non-negotiable.
- **Light Grey** is used for section backgrounds to create gentle visual separation.

### Accessibility

> ⚠️ All text must meet **WCAG AA** contrast standards. Never use white text on Signal Orange backgrounds for body copy — this fails contrast checks. Use Charcoal on orange for readable text, or white text on Charcoal backgrounds.

## 05 Typography

Typography is the backbone of our brand communication. We use two primary typefaces — a strong display font and a clean body font. Consistency in type creates trust.

### Font Stack

#### Bebas Neue — Display / Headlines
- **Source:** Google Fonts (Free)
- **Weight:** Regular (only weight available — all caps)
- **Use for:** Page headlines, hero text, section titles, logo wordmark, CTA buttons
- **Style:** All caps, tight leading, confident

#### DM Sans — Body / UI
- **Source:** Google Fonts (Free)
- **Weights:** 300 Light · 400 Regular · 500 Medium · 600 SemiBold
- **Use for:** Body copy, subheadings, navigation, UI text, captions, form labels
- **Style:** Clean, readable, modern

#### DM Mono — Data / Code
- **Source:** Google Fonts (Free)
- **Weights:** 400 Regular · 500 Medium
- **Use for:** Hex codes, technical specs, data tables, badges, eyebrow labels
- **Style:** Monospaced, precise

### Type Hierarchy

| Style | Size | Font | Colour |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| **H1 — Page Title** | 38–46pt | Bebas Neue | Charcoal or Signal Orange |
| **H2 — Section Title** | 22–26pt | Bebas Neue | Charcoal |
| **H3 — Subheading** | 13–15pt | DM Sans SemiBold | Charcoal |
| **Body** | 10–12pt | DM Sans Regular | Dark Grey `#3A3A3A` |
| **Caption / Label** | 8–9pt | DM Sans / DM Mono | Mid Grey `#6B6B6B` |
| **Data / Code** | 9–10pt | DM Mono | Charcoal or Signal Orange |

### Typography Rules

- Never use more than **two typefaces** in a single design
- Never use **system fonts** (Arial, Helvetica, Times) in designed materials
- Body copy line height: **1.6×** the font size
- Maximum body copy column width: **680px / 65 characters**
- Headings should use **tight leading** (1.1–1.15×)

## 06 Voice & Tone

Our voice is consistent across every touchpoint — website, ads, SMS, phone. Our tone shifts to match the moment: warmer and more empathetic when someone has just had an accident; more confident and direct in advertising.

### Writing Principles

**Lead with the benefit**
Tell people what they get, not what we do. "You won't pay a cent" not "We handle the claim".

**Plain English only**
No legal jargon, no insurance-speak. Write like you're explaining it to a friend.

**Be specific**
Use concrete details. "Free loan car within 30 minutes" beats "quick replacement vehicle".

**Active voice**
"We handle everything" not "Everything will be handled by our team".

**Short sentences**
Especially online. One idea per sentence. Make it scannable.

**Empathy first**
Acknowledge the situation before getting into the process. "We know this is stressful. Here's what happens next."

### Word Choices

| ✅ Say This | ❌ Not This |
|------------|-----------|
| Not at fault | Innocent party / Third party claimant |
| We handle everything | Our team will facilitate the process |
| Free loan car | Courtesy vehicle provided upon request |
| You won't pay a cent | No out-of-pocket expenses incurred |
| Call us now | Please contact our office at your convenience |
| Smash repair | Automotive bodywork restoration services |
| We deal with the insurer | Liaison with the relevant insurer will be undertaken |
| Zero cost to you | No financial obligation on your part |

### Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone |
|---------|------|
| **Website hero** | Bold, direct, benefit-first |
| **Website body** | Clear, calm, reassuring |
| **Google Ads** | Urgent, specific, action-oriented |
| **Social media** | Warm, helpful, conversational |
| **SMS / notifications** | Brief, friendly, action-oriented |
| **Phone / in-person** | Empathetic, calm, expert |
| **Blog content** | Informative, plain-English, helpful |

## 07 Imagery Guidelines

Photography and imagery should reinforce our brand values: trust, calm, and resolution. We show the positive outcome — not the accident itself.

### Photography Rules

**Show relief, not distress**
Feature people who look relieved, calm, or satisfied — not panicked or upset. Our brand is about resolution, not the problem.

**Real people, real moments**
Avoid overly staged stock photography. Favour natural lighting, candid expressions, and authentic settings like workshops, car parks, and phone calls.

**Clean vehicles**
When showing vehicles, show restored, clean, finished cars — not damaged ones. The exception is before/after sequences used in testimonials.

**Warm, natural tones**
Photography should have warm, slightly desaturated tones. Avoid cold blue-tinted stock photos or overly bright white lighting.

**Diversity**
Australians are diverse. Our imagery should reflect a wide range of customers — different ages, backgrounds, and vehicle types.

### Photography Do's & Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| Show repaired, gleaming vehicles | Show damaged, crashed, or dented cars |
| Feature relieved, satisfied customers | Show distressed or upset people |
| Use warm, natural workshop lighting | Use cold, clinical, blue-tinted images |
| Show diverse ages and backgrounds | Use exclusively young or white subjects |
| Show authentic repair environments | Use overly staged, fake-looking stock |

### Icon & Illustration Style

Icons should be simple, filled, and rounded — never overly detailed or illustrated. Use a single colour per icon:

- **Signal Orange** `#FF5C00` — active, highlighted, featured
- **Mid Grey** `#6B6B6B` — inactive, secondary, supporting

**Recommended icon libraries:**
- [Lucide Icons](https://lucide.dev)
- [Phosphor Icons](https://phosphoricons.com)
- [Material Symbols](https://fonts.google.com/icons)

## 08 Do's & Don'ts

When in doubt, ask: *does this feel trustworthy, clear, and easy?*

### Overall Brand Behaviour

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| Lead every message with the customer benefit | Abbreviate to NFC or NAFC in customer-facing materials |
| Use the full brand name "Not at Fault Claims" | Use orange as a background colour for body text |
| Keep designs clean with generous white space | Clutter designs with too many competing elements |
| Use Signal Orange for CTAs and key highlights only | Use more than two typefaces in any single design |
| Maintain consistent tone across all channels | Use stock photos of damaged or crashed vehicles |
| Always include the tagline on hero banners | Sound clinical, legal, or insurance-like in copy |

### Digital & Social

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| Respond to all reviews within 24 hours | Post graphic accident or crash imagery |
| Use captions on all video content | Engage in disputes publicly on social media |
| Maintain consistent handle: `@notaatfaultclaims` | Use all-caps in social copy (except logo/headings) |
| Pin key service info to social profiles | Post without proofreading — trust depends on accuracy |
